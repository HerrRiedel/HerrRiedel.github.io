CES preferences, Pareto costs, fixed cost f — baseline vs counterfactual

Single industry. Continuum of firms with marginal costs distributed Pareto. CES price aggregator P. Melitz-style selection: firms with negative net profit exit, and the productivity cutoff is determined jointly with P in equilibrium. Outputs: sales and profits by productivity rank ω (0 = most productive), plus aggregate welfare changes.

Baseline

Counterfactual (CF)

Theorem 1. The most productive firms benefit from cost increases via the shape parameter (decreases in κ).
Theorem 2. No firm benefits (or is negatively affected) from changes in the scale parameter ψ0.
Marginal cost ⓘFirm-ω marginal cost: ψ(ω) = ψ0 · (1−ω)−1/κ. At ω = 0 (most productive), ψ = ψ0. As ω → 1, ψ → ∞ (Pareto tail). Sales ⓘFirm-ω revenue: r(ω) = Y · (1−ω)(σ−1)/κ / [C · (1−tα)], where C = κ/(κ+σ−1), α = κ+σ−1, t = ψ0/ψ̄. Equivalently r(ω) = Y · (μψω/P)1−σ. Net profit ⓘNet profit per firm: π(ω) = r(ω)/σ − f. Operating profit equals revenue/σ under CES with constant markup μ = σ/(σ−1); subtract fixed cost f. Y/P ⓘConsumer real income. P is the CES price aggregator over active firms; under CES, indirect utility from spending Y is exactly Y/P. Π ⓘAggregate net profit: Π = Y/σ − f·ω̄, where ω̄ is the mass of active firms. Operating profits sum to Y/σ under Dixit–Stiglitz; subtract total fixed costs paid by survivors.
Active mass ω̄ (base / CF) ⓘFraction of potential entrants with non-negative net profit. ω̄ = 1 − (ψ0/ψ̄)κ, where ψ̄ is the marginal-cost cutoff. ω̄ = 1 when f = 0 (no exit).
Aggregator P (base / CF) ⓘCES price aggregator: P = (∫ pω1−σ dω)1/(1−σ) over active firms. With CES markup μ = σ/(σ−1), pω = μψω.
Top ε in favor ⓘTop firm interval [0, ε] such that every firm in that range earns higher net profit under the counterfactual than under the baseline.
Δ% Y/P / Δ% Π ⓘΔ% Y/P: percent change in consumer real income (Y/P) from baseline to CF. Δ% Π: percent change in aggregate net profit.
Demand. CES demand system with elasticity σ over a unit mass of varieties. Expenditure Y is exogenous and spent entirely on the industry.

Firms. Monopolistic competition. Marginal costs ψ are distributed Pareto with shape κ and scale ψ0: G(ψ) = 1 − (ψ0/ψ)κ for ψ ≥ ψ0. Convergence of the price aggregator requires κ > σ − 1. Each active firm pays an operating fixed cost f.

Equilibrium. Optimal price is a constant markup over marginal cost, pω = μψω with μ = σ/(σ−1). Operating profit equals (Y/σ)·(μψ/P)1−σ. Net profit subtracts f; firms with negative net profit exit. The exit cutoff ψ̄ satisfies ψ̄/P = (1/μ)(σf/Y)1/(1−σ), and the CES price aggregator P depends on the surviving firms — so ψ̄ and P are determined as a fixed point of the system (each implies the other). With Pareto costs this reduces to a one-dimensional equation in t = ψ0/ψ̄, solved by bisection. Rank ω = G(ψω), so ω = 0 is the most productive firm.

Welfare. "Y/P" = consumer real income (CES indirect utility from spending Y). "Π" = aggregate net profit = Y/σ − f·ω̄. Both reported as % change CF vs baseline.

ε (top in favor). Firm interval [0, ε] of ranks with Δπω > 0 under the counterfactual.
