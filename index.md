  <p>
    I am a PhD candidate in Economics at UC San Diego, expected to graduate in 2027. My research is in macroeconomic theory and regulatory capture. My job market paper studies industry-wide regulations whose marginal burden collapses for productive firms: through a price aggregator, these regulations shift relative prices in their favor, raising market share and profits without requiring exemptions or discriminatory enforcement. I apply this framework to minimum wages, where it explains why firms already paying above the floor expand after increases and why the resulting rents masquerade as productivity gains in the data.
