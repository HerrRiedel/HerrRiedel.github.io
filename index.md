  <p>
    I am a PhD candidate in Economics at UC San Diego, expected to graduate in 2027. My research is in macroeconomic theory and regulatory capture. My job market paper studies industry-wide regulations whose marginal burden collapses for productive firms: through the price aggregator, these regulations shift relative prices in their favor, raising market share and profits without requiring exemptions, discriminatory enforcement, exit of rivals, or strategic interaction. Applied to minimum wages, the framework explains a well-documented puzzle: why do productivity, market share, and employment of firms already paying above the floor rise after minimum wage increases?
